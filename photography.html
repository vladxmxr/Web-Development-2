So you want to edit like me?

Then keep on reading!

I know a lot of people who watch my Instagram stories are always like "How on earth did you do that?!" Simple, it's called Nichi.

Nichi is an app that includes various different templates, that all fit a typical iPhone screen. It also features it's own stickers, filters, fonts and backgrounds, making it easier
to make a collage. Nichi is also available for Android.

"Omg your Instagram is like SOOO cool,
           what do you use to edit?"

Well HONEY I'm glad you asked.

When I take photos for my Instagram,
sometimes I would get into these really
weird moods where I'm like "Oooh!
Definitely going to change up my eye colour,
drop 25kg, smoothen out my ENTIRE skin"
and so on and so forth, that's old Vlad. New
Vlad likes to go 'au natural', where all I do is
put a filter on my photo and alter the exposures,
contrast, sharpening, and adding grain. I would
go through different phases of using HB2, to C8,
to J2(to all my fellow VSCO cam queens who
understand my lingo, I love you)... basically I
could never stick to the one filter. Lately I've
discovered just using A6 because it's such a nice
filter for more natural looks (depending how
natural you wanna go). As you can see here to
the left, there is the original photo on top, and
the edited on the bottom, and I have provided
my VSCO preset to achieve this filter to the right
(you are so totally welcome by the way).

So how exactly DO I whiten, add
detailing, smoothen my skin, etc.?

As shown on the left, you can see the original, the
whitened version, and then a whitened and edited
version. In order to achieve this look, I use FaceTune
(if you have not heard of this app before, stop reading
now, go out and breathe in some fresh 2019 air and re-
evaluate all your life choices IMMEDIATELY). FaceTune
has plenty of options, such as a re-sizing tool, a tones tool,
a patch tool and so on and so forth. Although this app is a whopper €4.49, it is probably the best iPhone app to use
for editing (although you can do all of this on photoshop
but who really wants to pay €20 a month for it??) For this photo in particular, I used the whitening tool to whiten the
background and my shirt, and I used the detailing tool to highlight my necklace and some of my moles. I then went onto VSCO cam and used the preset shown on the left-hand screen (no, I did not copy any of my filters from online,
I swear, please believe me).

And how DID I edit my Paris photos?

Well of course I used VSCO Cam for the filters (I used M5, for
these photos), adjusted the contrast, exposure and grain ever so
slightly, but how did I add those beautiful quotes? (I robbed them
from a Bratz-inspired Instagram page, please don't tell my momma,
she loves those quotes). Using the Helvetica-Neue font on the app
'phonto', changing the colour to yellow and adjusting the thickness
and shadowing of the font, I placed the text at the bottom of the pictures
before adding any other tweaks. As seen above for the rainbow effect, I
used a prism while photographing. All images were taken on my iPhone 8
(who even needs a DSLR camera nowadays??)
